Yaakov is running. He's running from his brother, who wants to kill him. He's alone. It's nighttime. He stops to sleep on the ground, with a rock for a pillow. And he has the most famous dream in the Torah.

> **וְהִנֵּה סֻלָּם מֻצָּב אַרְצָה וְרֹאשׁוֹ מַגִּיעַ הַשָּׁמָיְמָה וְהִנֵּה מַלְאֲכֵי אֱלֹהִים עֹלִים וְיֹרְדִים בּוֹ** &middot; "And behold, a ladder set on the ground, its top reaching the heavens, and behold, angels of God were going up and down on it" (Bereishit 28:12).

The order of the angels is weird. They go up, and then down. Not down, and then up.

You'd think if God is sending angels from heaven to earth, they'd come down first and go back up second. But the Torah says it the other way. Up, then down.

The chassidic masters read this as a kind of diagram of a life. The ladder is our life. And the spiritual traffic on it moves in both directions, in cycles, always. Up. Then down. Then up again. Then down again. That's the design.

We tend to imagine that spiritual life should only move in one direction — upward. We've had a good stretch; we think we've arrived. Then we have a bad week and it feels like a failure. Like we've lost the progress. Like we need to start over.

Yaakov's ladder quietly corrects this. The going down is not a correction to the going up. The going down is also part of being on the ladder. An angel that only goes up and never comes down isn't an angel traveling on a ladder at all — it's just floating.

The movement in both directions is the whole point. That's how you're actually on the thing.

Yaakov is at rock bottom here — alone, running for his life, sleeping on dirt. And God shows him, right at that moment: this is still the ladder. Down is part of it. You haven't fallen off. You haven't been forgotten. The angels are still moving. Keep sleeping.

When we wake up from a hard week and feel like we've lost something, it can help to remember the order in Yaakov's dream. Up, down. Up, down. Both are the ladder. You don't leave it by coming down. You leave it by not moving at all.

## Takeaway

When this week feels like a step down — it's not off the ladder. It's part of it. The movement in both directions is what makes it a ladder in the first place.
