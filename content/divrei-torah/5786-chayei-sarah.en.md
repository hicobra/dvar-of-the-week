Eliezer is sent to find a wife for Yitzchak. He doesn't just ask God to show him the right girl. He designs a test.

> **וְהָיָה הַנַּעֲרָ אֲשֶׁר אֹמַר אֵלֶיהָ הַטִּי־נָא כַדֵּךְ וְאֶשְׁתֶּה וְאָמְרָה שְׁתֵה וְגַם־גְּמַלֶּיךָ אַשְׁקֶה** &middot; "Let it be that the girl I say to, 'please tip down your jug so I may drink,' and she says, 'drink, and I will also water your camels' — that is the one" (Bereishit 24:14).

The test is that she'll offer to water his camels.

Sounds simple. Until you remember that Eliezer has ten camels. A thirsty camel drinks about twenty-five gallons of water. So watering ten of them is roughly two hundred and fifty gallons. All hauled by hand, from a well, in a clay jug, one trip at a time. That's not a small favor. That's about an hour of hard work, at least.

This isn't a test of whether the girl is nice to strangers. It's a test of whether she has a *ceiling*.

Most of us are generous up to a point. We offer help. We pitch in. We do a kind thing. But there's usually a moment — somewhere between "sure, I'll help carry in the bags" and "wait, you want me to help carry *more* bags?" — where we hit our limit. That's the normal shape of human kindness. It has edges.

Eliezer is looking for someone whose kindness doesn't have edges. Someone who sees ten camels and doesn't think "well, one maybe, I'll be nice to this traveler." Someone who just... starts pouring. And keeps pouring. Until the camels are done.

Rivka shows up and does exactly that.

I think about this every time I catch the edge of my own generosity. That moment when a small favor turns into a bigger one, and I can feel myself starting to do the calculation. The real test of chesed isn't whether you'll start — most people will start. It's how long you'll go once you realize it's more than you expected.

Rivka didn't know Eliezer was watching. She didn't know it was a test. She didn't even know him. She just kept going until the job was done.

That's the quality Eliezer was sent to find. And it's the one the Torah wanted us to meet first.

## Takeaway

The real test of kindness isn't whether you'll start. It's whether you'll keep going once it's more than you signed up for. This week, when a small favor turns into a bigger one — don't stop pouring.
