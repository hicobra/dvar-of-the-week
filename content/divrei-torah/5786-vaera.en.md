Moshe comes back to Bnei Yisrael after his first confrontation with Pharaoh went badly. The people are suffering more, not less. They don't listen to him. Pharaoh doubled the workload. Moshe himself is frustrated. And then God speaks.

> **וְהוֹצֵאתִי אֶתְכֶם... וְהִצַּלְתִּי אֶתְכֶם... וְגָאַלְתִּי אֶתְכֶם... וְלָקַחְתִּי אֶתְכֶם לִי לְעָם** &middot; "I will take you out... I will save you... I will redeem you... I will take you to Me as a nation" (Shemot 6:6–7).

Four verbs. Four separate actions. Chazal call these the *arba lashonot shel geulah* — the four expressions of redemption. It's why we drink four cups of wine at the seder.

But notice what the four don't say. They don't say "I will redeem you, period." They say: *out*. Then: *saved*. Then: *redeemed*. Then: *taken as a nation*. Four different things, in four different stages.

I think this is worth pausing on, because it's a kind of theology we don't usually articulate. The Torah is describing redemption as a process that has phases. The going-out is not the same as the being-saved, which is not the same as being-redeemed, which is not the same as being-taken. Each is a different step, and each step has its own work.

You can be physically out and still not saved. You can be saved and still not redeemed. You can be redeemed and still not yet a nation. Freedom, in the Torah's telling, is not a single door you walk through. It's a sequence you move through. And if you're honest about your own life, you probably already know this.

A lot of the frustration we feel in our own personal stuff comes from thinking we should be further along than we are. We got out — why aren't we saved yet? We got saved — why don't we feel redeemed? The Torah is saying: that's how it goes. Step by step. Stage by stage.

Moshe came back discouraged. God's response is, in essence: let me tell you the shape of this thing. It's four stages, not one. Drink four cups, not one, because that's what freedom actually looks like.

## Takeaway

Redemption isn't a door. It's a staircase. If you're not where you'd hoped to be, check which stage you're actually in. The Torah says each stage has its own name and its own time.
